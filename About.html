Hi, I'm Junu Kasim

An engineer with over seven years of experience in the semiconductor industry, including four years specializing in physical design. My career journey has taken me through diverse roles and projects, from hands-on tapeouts at advanced technology nodes to academic research and design exploration.

Most recently, I completed my Master’s degree in Integrated Circuits and Systems Design, where I deepened my expertise in both analog and digital design. During this program, I gained extensive hands-on experience through practical labs using industry-standard EDA tools. The curriculum offered me a solid foundation in mixed-signal design, covering full-custom analog layout, gate-level digital design in VHDL, and system-level modeling.

For my Master’s thesis, I had the opportunity to work with Infineon Technologies, investigating a state-of-the-art hybrid switched-capacitor DC-DC converter. This research helped me sharpen my skills in analog circuit blocks like gate drivers, level shifters, and dead-time generators, while also improving my simulation and modeling capabilities.

Prior to my thesis, I worked as a Research Intern, focusing on automation in analog layout. I explored MAGICAL, an open-source analog layout generator, and worked on porting a design in TSMC 65nm technology to this platform — gaining valuable experience in design rule translation and layout generation.

Before pursuing my Master’s, I spent four years as a Physical Design Engineer, contributing to multiple successful tapeouts. I began my career at AMD, where I worked on block-level designs at TSMC 7nm technology. I later joined MediaTek, contributing to projects across nodes ranging from 55nm down to 5nm. My responsibilities included the full flow from netlist to GDSII, with a strong emphasis on meeting timing, area, and power goals. I also had the opportunity to lead multiple blocks to tapeout, further strengthening my project ownership and technical leadership skills.

Over the years, I’ve developed a strong ability to work under pressure, manage tight deadlines, and adapt quickly to new environments and challenges. One of the most defining decisions of my career was leaving a full-time job to pursue higher education — a step that not only expanded my technical skill set but also helped me grow personally by working and studying across different countries and cultures.

I am now eager to apply this blend of academic and professional experience to real-world design challenges, and I remain committed to continuous learning and pushing the boundaries of what’s possible in semiconductor technology.
